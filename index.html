I approach data science from a pragmatic experience, and believe simple, interpretable models, are more robust to outliers and generalize better, while having practical utility as decision support systems. In as much, I am more concerned about issues arising out of noisy and lack of ground truth data, which motivates the exploitation of unsupervised, or semi supervised techniques, while at the same alleviating issues arising out of data sparsity with novel featurization for the downstream task.

Hi! I am Chirag, a Research Masters student at the Language Technologies Institute in the School of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University.

I am advised by Prof. Artur Dubrawski and primarily work on scalable techniques for Semi-Supervised Learning over massive graphs at the Machine Learning Department's Auton Lab.
